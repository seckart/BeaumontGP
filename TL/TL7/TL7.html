Advocacy and Influence TL7

 

Nurse leaders, with clinical nurse input, use trended data to acquire necessary resources to support the care delivery system(s).

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, where a nurse leader, with clinical nurse input, used trended data to acquire necessary resources to support the care delivery system(s).

 

 

Example One: Acquisition of Additional Equipment Resources in the Surgical Suite

TL7, Surgical Volume Increase, 2013-2016

 

Many factors contributed to the increased volume at BGP, including:

  • Physician preference due to efficiency of clinical nurses
  • Recruitment of new physicians
  • Flexibility of boarding procedures
  • Patient request and satisfaction

 

The flexibility of boarding has been a great enticement for physicians to bring their patients to BGP. (Boarding time is when the patient is scheduled for a surgical procedure.) Staffing is based on the physician schedules to ensure that the boarding time is acceptable to the patient, physician and hospital. As surgical volumes increase, so does the requirement of resources for both staffing and equipment. Equipment needs are identified in several ways.

  • Equipment requirements are indicated on the physician boarding request form.
  • The clinical nurse who functions as the Service Line Coordinator reviews the boarding request and preliminarily determines what equipment is required for the surgical procedure.
  • Surgical Services nurse leaders review equipment needs with clinical nurses who will be directly working in the case in the Surgical Suite. 
  • The equipment needs are discussed during the 1 p.m. Equipment Huddles every day. The huddles are attended by the clinical nurse Service Line Coordinators, Material Management and Central Processing Department. The purpose of the huddle is to identify the equipment needs for upcoming Operating Room (OR) procedures to ensure the appropriate equipment is available for the surgery. If there is an issue, the clinical nurse Service Line Coordinators inform the nurse leader.

 

Linda Witt, MA, BSN, RN, ONC, Director Surgical Services, supports and encourages communication between nursing leaders, clinical nurses and all members of the surgical team. Interdisciplinary Huddle is held every day at 10 a.m. and is attended by Witt; Kathleen Vandelinder, BSN, RN, CNOR, Associate Nurse Manager, Surgical Services; clinical nurses; a physician; a CRNA; a Central Processing clerk; and a midlevel provider. The purpose of the huddle is to review the current day’s schedule, surgical cases that are three days out and any outstanding issues with future procedures that could delay or cancel the case.

 

Equipment Huddle is conducted at 1 p.m. every day and is attended by clinical nurses, a Materials Management clerk and Central Processing clerks, and Vandelinder. The purpose of the huddle is to ensure all equipment is available for future procedures. The clinical nurse is ultimately responsible for ensuring that everything that is needed for a scheduled surgical procedure has been secured. 

 

Clinical nurses also work closely with Central Processing Department (CPD) staff and Materials Management staff to ensure equipment needs are met. Equipment coordination can be very complex because of constant changes in the procedural environment. The Surgical Suite is responsive to constant changes in scheduling and physician requests.

 

Clinical Nurse Input
Stephanie Zimmer, BSN, RN, clinical nurse, Surgical Services, also serves as the Service Line Coordinator for General, Gynecological, and Robotics. The Service Line Coordinator’s function is to review all upcoming procedures, coordinate equipment needs for current and future cases, and review and update the Doctors Preference Cards (DPC). 

 

Zimmer met with Witt on November 3, 2016 and explained the issues that were evolving. They discussed:

  • That the Loner Tray would no longer be available
  • More GYN physicians requesting instruments
  • What to do to avoid scheduling conflicts
  • Obtaining quotes for instruments

(Evidence TL7-1, Notes from November 3, 2016 Zimmer Conservation and Quote)

 

Trended Data
The availability of equipment and instrumentation in OR is critical, and it is also imperative that immediate use steam sterilization (IUSS), or “flashing,” is kept to a minimum since it is not intended for routine use.

 

Flashing is used to quickly sterilize surgical instruments when they are needed as soon as possible, such as when a patient is on the OR table and a one of a kind piece of equipment is dropped. Flashing is approved for minimal usage according to the Association of periOperative Registered Nurses (AORN), and it should be done only when there is not sufficient time to process by the preferred terminal sterilization. 

 

Steam flush pressure pulse (SFPP) is performed in CPD and is the recommended way of sterilizing instruments. IUSS should not be used as a substitute for sufficient instrument inventory according to the Guideline for Sterilization in the AORN Guidelines for Perioperative Practices, 2015 Edition.

 

In late 2016, Zimmer noted that the number of GYN TruClear cases in the Surgical Suite had been increasing over the last two years and met with Witt to discuss the need to purchase more instrumentation. Zimmer reviewed the GYN surgical volume with Witt and explained that it had been increasing over the past two years. The volume rose from 20 cases in first quarter 2015 to 31 cases in fourth quarter 2016, a 55% increase in two years.

TL7, TruClear Case Volume

 

The increase in the volume of GYN TruClear cases led to an increase in the flashing of surgical instruments to ensure their availability for the surgeon. There will always be flashing, although it should never exceed two flashes per day. Zimmer explained to Witt that the increase in volume had led to an increase in the number of flashes. Flashing is a safe and quick sterilization process, but it is recommended that an instrument not be flashed more than twice day. 

 

Witt reviewed the data and determined that the number of flashings increased from 19 in first quarter 2015 to 38 in fourth quarter 2016, a 50% increase in two years. The number of days on which the flashing recommendation was exceeded was also increasing. Zimmer expressed concern to Witt about the increased incidents of flashing.

To maintain the integrity and useful life of instrumentation, it is recommended that instrument sets should be used and sterilized for no more than two use cycles within a 24-hour period. The TruClear instrumentation set is used for operative hysteroscopy, polypectomy and myomectomy. 

 

On March 20, 2017, Zimmer began to express the need for more TruClear instrumentation sets, both verbally and through email communications. Zimmer expressed concern that BGP “was so close to cancelling cases, in fact more than a dozen times.” (Evidence TL7-3, Email from Zimmer to Witt)

 

On March 31, 2017, Zimmer sent Witt an email outlining the difficulties that might arise in April 2017. Zimmer noted that there were 11 TruClear cases scheduled in April, which would potentially lead to issues on April 11 and 12. She wrote to Witt, “On April 11th and 12th, we will face an issue of cancellation or delay if the loaner is returned, or our sets face repair. Any word on obtaining the difficult capital to purchase just one small set to alleviate our continual, current issue.” (Evidence TL7-4, Emails from Zimmer to Witt)

 

Acquired Resources
TruClear instrumentation is quite costly, at nearly $21,000 per set. Zimmer repeatedly communicated the need for more TruClear instrumentation sets, both verbally and in written form, and stated her concern about using the IUSS process more often than is recommended. As capital dollars are in great demand, Witt carefully evaluates and determines the greatest need for resources. 

 

After numerous communications from Zimmer, Witt sought final approval for the purchase of an additional TruClear instrument set. Final approval for capital purchases require a compelling rationale to justify the purchase. The purchase would result in a decrease of IUSS rates and provide great relief to the clinical nurses by ensuring the proper instrumentation would be available for the patients. 

 

Based on the information provided by Zimmer, Witt approached Rick Swaine, MBA, MSF, CPA, Hospital President, to obtain immediate capital funding for the TruClear instrument set. Swaine approved funding for the purchase, and Witt purchased two sets of instruments.

 

On April 26, 2017, a purchase order was created for one set of TruClear surgical instruments, which was received in May 2017. The second purchase ordered was entered on May 30, 2017, and BGP Surgical Services expects a June 2017 delivery.
(Evidence TL7-5, Purchase Orders and Email Regarding Purchases)

 

Care Delivery System
The nurse leader, with clinical nurse input, used trended data to support the purchase of additional surgical instruments to support the delivery of required patient care in the Surgical Suite. With the extra equipment there is less need for flashing, which it is recommended be done only twice per day. Ensuring that adequate equipment is available to meet patient care needs in a safe environment supports the patient-centered care delivery system of Beaumont Grosse Pointe.
