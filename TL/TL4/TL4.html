TL4

 

Advocacy and Influence TL4

 

The CNO is a strategic partner in the organization's decision-making.

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, of the CNO's involvement in the organization's decision-making (not involving technology).
    AND

  • Provide one example, with supporting evidence, of the CNO's involvement in the organization’s technology decision-making.

 

 

Example One: CNO Involvement in Organization Decision Making – Transitioning a Long-Term Care Facility Area into a Medical/Surgical Unit  

 

The Administrative Team, which is the highest-level committee at Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP), meets weekly with the following individuals attending:

  • Rick Swaine, MBA, MSF, CPA, Hospital President
  • Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer
  • Christine Stesney-Ridenour, MBA, Vice President and Chief Operating Officer
  • Kristen Cavender, BSIE, MBA, CSSBB, Administrative Director
  • Robert Kilpatrick, BSRS, RTR, Administrative Director
  • Maria Miller, Controller BGP
  • Donna Hoban, MD, Senior Vice President and Physician in Chief

 

The group’s focus is to make executive, strategic and operational decisions for BGP, addressing issues including strategic planning, financial performance, human resources, quality, regulatory and other executive issues. The CNO is an active and equal partner in all organizational decisions at BGP.

 

One issue that the team discussed was transitioning a long-term care facility area into a medical/surgical unit at BGP. The following describes the CNO’s involvement in the planning and development of the area.

 

CNO Involvement – Planning
During a January 2015 Vice Presidents meeting, which is a subgroup of the Administrative Team, the group discussed backfilling 3 North. This meeting is attend by Stewart, Swain, Stesney-Ridenour and Hoban. An Evaluation Team was appointed at this meeting, with Stewart assigned as the senior executive sponsor to assist in leading it. The team consisted of:  

  • Anne Stewart MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer
  • Kristen Cavender, BSIE, MBA, CSSBB, Administrative Director
  • Christine Grumeretz, Facilities Architect Planner, REDP Planning
  • Stephanie Magina, Senior Project Manager, Project Development BGP – Facilities
  • Maria Miller, Controller BGP

 

The Evaluation Team, which Stewart was a member of, was going to evaluate four proposals regarding what would occupy the 3 North space. The proposed uses were:

  • Inpatient rehabilitation unit
  • Medical/surgical telemetry private room unit
  • Geriatric-psych unit
  • Continue the lease with the long-term acute care hospital (LTACH) 

(Evidence TL4-1, Agenda VP Meeting, January 7, 2015 and Email Communication)

 

The Evaluation Team was to evaluate each proposal and present their recommendation to the BGP Hospital Administrative Team, which CNO Stewart is a member of, on February 10, 2015. Stewart, representing hospital administration, led the Evaluation Team and was involved in researching each proposal since nursing plays an integral role in three of the four options. 

 

Inpatient Rehab
Stewart met with Grumeretz to discuss The Joint Commission and State of Michigan requirements for an inpatient rehab unit, and she held conference calls with the Rehabilitation nursing leaders from Beaumont Hospital Royal Oak and Beaumont Hospital Troy. Following the calls and further investigation into the code requirements, the team’s recommendation was to not pursue an inpatient rehabilitation unit because of space constraints. The space would allow for 8-10 rehab beds, which the staffing requirements would make it fiscally difficult to maintain.  

 

Medical/Surgical Telemetry Unit
With many patients waiting in the Intensive Care Unit and Emergency Center for telemetry monitoring, another proposal was to open a medical/surgical telemetry private room unit on which all beds would be telemetry capable.

 

Telemetry monitoring service (TMS) at BGP is done remotely from Beaumont Royal Oak. Stewart contacted Monique Brand, BA, Cardiology Manager at Beaumont Royal Oak, to discuss the feasibility of adding telemetry monitoring beds at BGP. Brand informed Stewart that there was no additional capacity to expand the TMS at BGP due to the space constraints, and she suggested that services could be added if BGP moved TMS to another location. 

 

Geriatric-Psych Unit
On January 20, 2015, Stewart, Miller and Cavender held a conference call with nursing colleagues at Beaumont Hospital Farmington Hills, which was the only hospital in Beaumont Health that had an Inpatient geriatric-psych unit.  

 

On the call from Beaumont Farmington Hills was Dahlia Klein, MSN, RN, CCRN, CCM, Director of Nursing; Terry Slavin, Director of Reimbursement; and Regina Doxtader, Controller. It became clear early in the call that a geriatric-psych service was operationally and administratively labor intensive because of the many regulations and personnel requirements, and that BGP did not have the breadth and depth of expertise to pursue this option. 

 

Long-Term Acute Care Hospital
Multiple meetings were held with Select Specialty Hospital to discuss their plans for the following year. Select Specialty wanted to renew the lease providing they could obtain more administrative space and another five-year contract. As BGP was unable to meet either condition, it was decided that continuing with an LTACH was not feasible.

 

On February 7, 2015, Stewart, Cavender, Grumeretz, Magina and Miller met to discuss strategies and recommendations for the February 10, 2015 meeting with the Administrative Ream when they would present their recommendation on what would backfill 3 North once the LTACH left.

 

Organizational Decision Making
The Administrative Team met on February 10, 2015 to review the proposals from the Evaluation Team. (Evidence TL4-2, Evaluation Team Proposal and Agenda from Administrative Team Meeting) Based on the information they gathered and an increasing volume, the Evaluation Team recommended converting the Select Specialty space into a medical/surgical private room unit. This would give patients privacy and help with patient flow from the Emergency Center. With patient volumes increasing, it was difficult for the unit clinical nurses to match patients by sex, isolation status or other types of fit, which takes valuable time and resources to accommodate as patients are transferred.   

 

During the next six months, discussions were held with various individuals regarding the renovation and capital needs to re-establish 3 North as a medical/surgical unit. (Evidence TL4-3, Request for Capital Requirements) 

 

As the CNO/Administrator for 3 North, Stewart was instrumental in advocating for capital dollars for its renovation. Stewart was very aware of issues related to the placement of admitted patients, and that the addition of 21 private beds would allow for a smoother transition of patients from the Emergency Center to the inpatient units.

 

The decision regarding the backfill of 3 North had not been made at this point, so Stewart began lobbying for the area to become an all private medical/surgical area. In August 2015, Beaumont Health Corporate offices began questioning the BGP Administrative Team about the recommendation to add beds at BGP when the occupancy report often reported the average daily census at around 50% occupied. 

 

On paper, it did not look like BGP needed additional patient care areas, so a special meeting was set in

November 2015 with Beaumont Health Chief Financial Officer John Keuten and the BGP Administrative Team, including Stewart, to discuss the Administrative Team’s desire to opt out of renewing the Select Specialty lease. 

 

Stewart, Swain, Hoban and Stesney-Ridenour presented an Occupancy Summary to put into perspective the hospital’s true capacity and occupancy challenges. (Evidence TL4-4, Occupancy Summary, November 25, 2015)

Following this meeting, Keuten supported the BGP Administrative Team’s recommendation of adding beds at BGP.

 

In December 2015, following several discussions with Select Specialty, a final decision was made for Select Specialty to vacate BGP in June 2016. 

 

In February 2016, BGP hospital administration was approached with a proposal to move another long-term care facility, Vibra, to the area vacated by Select Specialty. Vibra has a Joint Venture with the legacy Oakwood Hospital System, which became part of Beaumont Health in 2014. Because Vibra had a history with the system, this became a dilemma. The idea of Vibra moving into the area was met with resistance, especially from nursing, and Stewart was concerned about moving another LTACH into the area based on input from nursing staff and leadership.

 

During summer 2016, many discussions were held between BGP, Beaumont Health and Vibra regarding moving the LTACH into BGP.

 

An informal meeting between Steward and Swaine was held on September 1, 2016, and a final decision was made that the unit would become a 21-private bed medical/surgical unit caring for patients with medical/surgical and neurological diagnoses. Renovations and updating began in October 2016, with a projected occupancy in first quarter 2017.

 

Example Two: CNO Involvement in Organizational Technology Decision Making – Computer Replacement for Beaumont Grosse Pointe

 

Background
Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer at Beaumont Grosse Pointe (BGP), is a member of the BGP Administrative Team, which includes:

  • Rick Swaine, MBA, MSF, CPA, Hospital President
  • Anne Stewart, MBA, BSN, RNC, NE-BC, Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer
  • Kristen Cavender, BSIE, MBA, CSSBB, Vice President and Chief Operating Officer
  • Robert Kilpatrick, BSRS, RTR, Administrative Director
  • Maria Miller, Controller BGP

 

During many of the Nurse Leader Forums in early 2016, Stewart heard about the condition of the computers on the nursing units through the hospital, referred to as “WOWs,” or portable workstations on wheels. The WOWs are located throughout the hospital and are used by many different departments and individuals, including nurses, physicians, respiratory therapists, and care management, and pharmacy personnel, to document in the electronic medical record. 

 

Although the equipment was still functional, the clinical nurses reported that:

  • The computers were always broken and took too long to get fixed.
  • The computers were slow and would lock up.
  • The screens were dimly lit and too small to read easily.

 

Based on the feedback from the clinical nurses, Stewart spoke with Information Technology (IT)about the process for obtaining new computers for the organization. IT responded that the computers are now a capital item and that capital dollars are required to purchase new ones.  

 

Organizational Decision Making
In preparation for the opening of a new Surgical Intensive Care Unit (SICU), Stewart asked the clinical nurses in the Critical Care Unit (CCU) to trial a new computer and workstation. The new computer addressed all the issues that were identified by the clinical nurses, and they recommended purchasing them for use organization-wide.

 

The computers were purchased in March 2016 for a trial of the hardware, with the understanding that if the CCU clinical nurses thought they were acceptable they would be purchased for the new SICU and then hospital-wide once the capital dollars were approved. (Evidence TL4-5, Quotes for Computer, Workstations and PO)

 

During the Administrative Team’s weekly meeting on September 27, 2016, Stewart requested that dollars be allocated in the capital budget to purchase new computers and workstations for the organization. The capital budget allocation is decided by all the members of the Administrative Team, who decide how the capital dollars that are allocated to BGP from the Corporate Office are distributed.

 

During this meeting, Stewart shared the clinical staff’s feedback about the condition of the WOWs and the perceived lack of available computers. She also shared the CCU clinical nurses’ feedback about the computers that were trialed in the unit and would be purchased for the new SICU. (Evidence TL4-6, Administrator’s Weekly Meeting Minutes, September 27, 2016)

 

Stewart was successful in advocating for the new computers. This new, state of the art technology will meet the needs of all clinical staff members who use the computers for research and documentation in the electronic medical record. The 2017 capital budget was completed, and $330,000 was allocated to purchase new WOWs in third quarter 2017. (Evidence TL4-7, 2017 Capital Budget Allocation)

 

In January 2017, BGP opened a 21-bed medical/surgical unit, 3 North East. The input from the CCU clinical nurses was used to support Stewart’s decision to purchase the new WOWs and computers for this new unit as well, and the staff loves them. (Evidence TL4-8, PO and Email March 2, 2017 regarding Staff’s Opinion of Computer)

 

CNO Involvement in Planning
The issue of the computers was brought up during a Culture of Safety Town Hall on the Critical Care Stepdown Unit (CCSU) on January 16, 2017. The clinical staff said they continued to struggle with the computers, as they were always broken and the same computer would sometimes have to be sent down as many as six times for repair. After hearing these comments from the staff and how the issues continued to affect patient care, Stewart contacted Kori Ogden, Equipment Manager for Beaumont Health, to discuss the issue.

 

Stewart negotiated a plan to address the issue of broken computer technology:

  • Ogden would come to BGP to talk with the staff.
  • CCSU would be prioritized for computer refresh.
  • IT would ensure that all computers are working before returning.
  • Stewart arranged for Subra Sripada, Executive Vice President, Chief Transformation Officer and Chief Information Officer, to meet with the CCSU staff regarding the computer issues.

(Evidence TL4-9, Culture of Safety Town Hall Minutes)

 

After the Culture of Safety Town Hall, it was apparent that immediate action was required to correct this problem. Stewart requested from the IT department a status of all the computers and WOWs at BGP, and in a meeting with the CCSU Sripada said BGP would be the priority for computer replacement. 

 

The WOW and PC Refresh plan was put into place on February 27, 2017, and IT was going to do a complete inventory of all the computers and WOWs at BGP. Stewart shared her concern with Susan Grant, DNP, RN, FAAN, NEA-BC, Executive Vice President and Chief Nursing Officer, Beaumont Health, who told Stewart that a WOW Refresh Analysis was completed for all of Beaumont Health and would be shared at a future Nurse Executive Council meeting. 

 

All Beaumont Health CNOs and Nursing Vice Presidents sit on the system-wide Nurse Executive Council, which meets to discuss nursing issues. The Computer WOW Inventory and Analysis was presented at its March 30, 2017 meeting. (Evidence TL4-10, WOW and PC Refresh Grosse Pointe, 2017 WOW Refresh Analysis, and Nurse Executive Council Minutes March 30, 2017)

 

As the process of replacing all the computers and WOWs at BGP continues, Stewart has oversight and receives monthly updates on the progress of the computer purchases at BGP.
